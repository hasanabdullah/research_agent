## Evaluation Framework

Each idea scored on:
- **Speed to $10K/mo**: How fast can you realistically hit $10K MRR? (1-5, 5=fastest)
- **Solo Feasibility**: Can one person build and run this? (1-5, 5=easiest)
- **Defensibility**: What stops copycats? (1-5, 5=strongest moat)
- **Capital Required**: How much $ to start? (1-5, 5=lowest cost)
- **Scalability**: Does it get easier over time? (1-5, 5=most scalable)

## IDEA #1: Micro-SaaS (Vertical Niche Tool)

**What it is:** A narrow SaaS product solving one specific pain point for a well-defined audience (e.g., scheduling for dog groomers, invoice tracking for freelance photographers, compliance checklists for HOAs). Priced $29-$199/month.

**Revenue model:** Monthly/annual subscriptions. Self-serve signup, credit card on file.

**Startup cost:** $500-$3,000 (hosting, domain, Stripe fees). 2-4 months of full-time dev work for MVP. If using no-code/low-code, potentially launchable in 4-8 weeks.

**Path to $10K/month:**
- At $49/mo → 205 customers needed
- At $99/mo → 102 customers needed  
- At $199/mo → 51 customers needed
- Realistic timeline: 9-18 months to $10K MRR (SoftwareSeni 2025 data)
- Only 15% of micro-SaaS reach $10K-$100K MRR; 50% plateau at $1K-$10K

**Moat:** Switching costs (data lock-in), deep integrations with niche workflows, community/brand in a small market. Larger companies won't bother entering a $2M TAM.

**Skills required:** Software development (or no-code proficiency), basic marketing, customer discovery, domain knowledge of the niche.

**Real examples:**
- **Bannerbear** (Jon Yongfook): API for auto-generating images. $630K+ ARR, solo founder. Pricing $49-$399/mo.
- **Plausible Analytics** (Marko Saric & Uku Täht): Privacy-first analytics. $100K+ MRR. Positioned against Google on one dimension.
- **Carrd** (AJ): One-page website builder. $1M+ ARR solo. Deliberately limited scope.
- **Seline.so** (Kostya Nesterovich): Privacy analytics. $600 MRR in 6 months.
- **supastarter** (Jonathan Wilke): SaaS starter kit. $10K MRR after 2 years.

**Risks:**
- 30% of micro-SaaS never reach $1K MRR (2025 data)
- Distribution is the hard part, not building
- Churn can kill you — 5%+ monthly churn means you're on a treadmill
- AI tools making it easier for competitors to clone your product

**Scores:** Speed: 2 | Solo: 4 | Defensibility: 3 | Capital: 5 | Scalability: 5

## IDEA #2: Productized Service (Design/Dev/Marketing-as-a-Service)

**What it is:** Package a high-value skill into a fixed-price, subscription-based service. Instead of billing hourly, offer "unlimited design requests for $4,995/month" or "website speed optimization for $500 one-time." Combines the fast revenue of freelancing with the scalability of a product.

**Revenue model:** Monthly retainers or fixed-price packages. Some models use subscription ("unlimited requests" with queue management).

**Startup cost:** $0-$500 (website, portfolio, tools). You're selling your time/skills, so the main cost is opportunity cost.

**Path to $10K/month:**
- **High-ticket model** ($2K-$5K/client): 2-5 clients = $10K. Fastest path.
- **Mid-ticket** ($500/project): 20 clients/month = $10K. Requires marketing engine.
- **DesignJoy model** ($4,995/mo unlimited): 2 clients = $10K. Premium positioning.
- Realistic timeline: 1-4 months if you have existing skills and network.

**Moat:** Personal brand, reputation, client relationships, portfolio of results. Process/SOPs that enable consistent quality. Weak moat — easy to copy the model.

**Skills required:** The core skill being productized (design, dev, copywriting, SEO, video editing). Sales ability. Client management.

**Real examples:**
- **DesignJoy** (Brett Williams): Solo designer, $1.5M+ revenue. $4,995/mo unlimited design.
- **KENJI ROI** (Danny Carlson): Amazon listing optimization. Scaled to 7 figures.
- **WP Speed Fix**: WordPress speed optimization. $500/fix productized service.
- **Photologo**: Custom logo design at $49/each, high-volume model.

**Risks:**
- You're trading time for money (even with productization, capacity is limited)
- Burnout risk at high volume
- Client management overhead
- Hard to sell if you lack a portfolio or credibility
- Can be a stepping stone to SaaS, not an endpoint

**Scores:** Speed: 5 | Solo: 4 | Defensibility: 2 | Capital: 5 | Scalability: 2

## IDEA #3: AI-Powered SaaS (AI-Native Product)

**What it is:** A SaaS product where AI/LLMs are the core value proposition — not a wrapper around ChatGPT, but a product that uses AI to solve a specific workflow problem. Examples: AI video generation, AI-powered data analysis, AI customer support.

**Revenue model:** Subscription + usage-based pricing (common pattern: base subscription + per-unit charges for AI compute).

**Startup cost:** $1,000-$5,000 (higher due to API costs during development and testing). LLM API costs: $0.01-$0.10 per request depending on model.

**Path to $10K/month:**
- At $99/mo → 102 customers
- At $49/mo + $0.05/use → blended higher ARPU
- **AutoShorts.ai** (Eric Smith): Hit $113K+ MRR months after launch — AI video for marketers
- Timeline: 6-18 months, but can be much faster with viral product-market fit

**Moat:** Proprietary training data, fine-tuned models, workflow integration, UX polish on top of commodity AI. The AI itself isn't the moat — the product experience is.

**Skills required:** Software development, AI/ML understanding (prompt engineering at minimum), product design, understanding of the target workflow.

**Real examples:**
- **AutoShorts.ai** (Eric Smith): $113K+ MRR. AI-driven video creation for marketers.
- **Jasper AI**: AI copywriting, reached $75M ARR (but raised VC)
- **Numerous AI**: AI for spreadsheets. Solo-ish team, growing MRR.
- Case study from IndieHackers: Founder added AI to existing B2B SaaS, went from $2K → $50K MRR in 8 months by adding AI data analysis features.

**Risks:**
- API costs can eat margins if not managed
- "Wrapper" risk — OpenAI/Anthropic can build your feature natively
- Oversaturated in many categories (AI writing, AI image gen)
- Model improvements can commoditize your advantage overnight
- Need to find niches where AI + domain expertise creates real differentiation

**Scores:** Speed: 3 | Solo: 3 | Defensibility: 2 | Capital: 4 | Scalability: 5

## IDEA #4: AI Agency / Consulting

**What it is:** Help businesses implement AI solutions — custom chatbots, workflow automation, data analysis pipelines, AI strategy consulting. Sell expertise in applying AI to business problems, not building AI products for consumers.

**Revenue model:** Project fees ($5K-$100K), monthly retainers ($2K-$25K/mo), or hybrid.

**Startup cost:** $0-$1,000 (website, tools). The investment is in your AI expertise and business development.

**Path to $10K/month:**
- 2 retainer clients at $5K/mo = $10K
- 1 project at $15K + 1 retainer at $3K = ~$10K (blended)
- Some agencies scaling from $0 to $40K/month in 90 days
- Timeline: 1-3 months with hustle and existing network

**Moat:** Expertise depth, case studies/portfolio, client relationships, industry specialization. Weak structural moat but strong execution moat.

**Skills required:** AI/ML knowledge (prompt engineering, fine-tuning, RAG pipelines), business consulting, sales, project management. Domain expertise in target industry is a major differentiator.

**Real examples:**
- Market size: $7.63B in 2025, projected $50.31B by 2030 (45.8% CAGR)
- Leading AI agencies report 90%+ revenue growth in H2 2024
- 51% of organizations actively exploring AI agent integration
- Margins: 70-90% depending on revenue stream

**Risks:**
- Feast-or-famine revenue cycle
- Client education burden (many don't know what AI can do)
- Project scope creep
- Rapid pace of AI change requires constant upskilling
- Competition from big consulting firms entering the space

**Scores:** Speed: 5 | Solo: 3 | Defensibility: 2 | Capital: 5 | Scalability: 2

## IDEA #5: Newsletter / Paid Media Business

**What it is:** Build an email newsletter with a loyal audience in a valuable niche, then monetize through sponsorships, paid subscriptions, affiliate revenue, and digital product sales. Platforms like Beehiiv, ConvertKit/Kit, and Substack make this accessible.

**Revenue model:** Multi-stream — sponsorships (CPM-based), paid subscriptions ($5-$20/mo), affiliate commissions, beehiiv boosts, digital product sales.

**Startup cost:** $0-$50/month (Beehiiv free tier to $99/mo). Primary cost is time to create content.

**Path to $10K/month:**
- **Sponsorship model**: 50K subscribers × $50 CPM × 4 issues/mo = $10K/mo
- **Paid subscription**: 1,000 subscribers at $10/mo = $10K/mo
- **Hybrid** (realistic): 25K free subs with sponsors ($4K) + 300 paid ($3K) + affiliates ($2K) + digital products ($1K) = $10K
- Beehiiv user reported $5K/mo from affiliates, ads, and subs (Dec 2025)
- Timeline: 6-18 months to build audience, faster with existing audience

**Moat:** Audience relationship, brand trust, unique voice/perspective, curated expertise. Strong moat once built — audiences are loyal and hard to steal.

**Skills required:** Writing/content creation, audience building, basic marketing, niche expertise. No coding needed.

**Real examples:**
- **Morning Brew**: Sold for $75M. Started as a solo newsletter.
- **The Hustle**: Acquired by HubSpot for ~$27M.
- **Milk Road** (Shaan Puri): Crypto newsletter, sold for ~$10M after 10 months.
- **Beehiiv creator**: $5K/mo from a single newsletter (Dec 2025)
- Beehiiv data: 1,000 subscribers can generate $100-$2,000+/mo depending on niche

**Risks:**
- Slow audience growth (takes months to years)
- Content treadmill — must publish consistently
- Sponsorship revenue is cyclical/seasonal
- Email deliverability issues
- Oversaturated in some niches (tech, marketing, AI)
- Need a genuinely differentiated angle

**Scores:** Speed: 2 | Solo: 5 | Defensibility: 4 | Capital: 5 | Scalability: 3

## IDEA #6: Online Course / Cohort-Based Education

**What it is:** Create and sell online courses or run live cohort-based programs teaching a valuable skill. Can range from self-paced video courses ($50-$500) to premium cohort programs ($500-$5,000+).

**Revenue model:** One-time course sales, cohort enrollment fees, or subscription access to a course library.

**Startup cost:** $100-$2,000 (recording equipment, course platform like Teachable, Kajabi, or Maven). Main cost is time to create content.

**Path to $10K/month:**
- **Self-paced course at $199**: 51 sales/month = $10K
- **Cohort program at $1,500**: 7 students/cohort, 1 cohort/month = $10.5K
- **Premium cohort at $3,000**: 4 students/month = $12K
- **Subscription library at $29/mo**: 345 subscribers = $10K
- E-learning market projected to reach ~$400B globally
- Timeline: 2-6 months to create and launch; revenue depends on existing audience

**Moat:** Personal brand, teaching quality, community around the course, results/testimonials. Network effects in cohort models.

**Skills required:** Domain expertise worth teaching, content creation, basic marketing, community management for cohorts. No coding needed.

**Real examples:**
- **Ali Abdaal**: Reportedly earns $5M+/year from courses and content
- **Tiago Forte** (Building a Second Brain): Premium cohort, $2M+/year
- **Maven**: Platform enabling $500-$5,000 cohort courses, many solo instructors hitting $10K+/month
- **Teachable**: Reports many creators earning $10K+/month, especially in B2B skills

**Risks:**
- Requires existing audience/credibility to sell effectively
- High refund rates on low-quality courses
- Content becomes outdated (especially in tech/AI)
- Market fatigue — "another online course" skepticism
- Completion rates are notoriously low (5-15%)
- Revenue is lumpy if launch-based rather than evergreen

**Scores:** Speed: 3 | Solo: 5 | Defensibility: 3 | Capital: 5 | Scalability: 4

## IDEA #8: Shopify App (Platform Marketplace SaaS)

**What it is:** Build an app for the Shopify App Store that helps merchants with a specific need — email marketing, upselling, inventory management, invoicing, analytics, etc. 5.6M+ Shopify stores create massive demand. Revenue share: 0% on first $1M earned, then 15% to Shopify.

**Revenue model:** Monthly subscriptions ($5-$299/mo), usage-based pricing, or freemium with paid tiers. Shopify handles billing.

**Startup cost:** $500-$5,000 (development time is the main cost). Shopify Partner account is free. Can build MVP in 2-4 weeks if focused.

**Path to $10K/month:**
- **ABConvert**: $0 → $1K MRR in 5 months → $10K in 12 months → $50K MRR by month 24
- **WideBundle**: Built in 14 days, $10K MRR in <6 months, now $55-57K MRR
- **OrderlyPrint**: Solo founder, $27K/month at 85% margin ($275K annual profit)
- At $29/mo → 345 paying merchants = $10K
- At $49/mo → 205 merchants = $10K
- Realistic timeline: 6-12 months to $10K MRR for the top ~10% of apps

**Revenue distribution (brutal reality):**
| Tier | % of Apps |
|------|-----------|
| $0 (zero reviews/revenue) | 35% |
| Under $1K/month | 54.5% |
| $1K-$10K/month | ~30-35% |
| $10K-$83K/month | ~10% |
| Over $83K/month ($1M+/yr) | 0.18% |

**Average Shopify app developer earns $93K/year; top 25% earn $167K/year.**

**Moat:** Reviews and ratings (social proof is critical), deep Shopify integration, merchant switching costs, app store SEO ranking. Strong moat once established — merchants hate switching apps.

**Skills required:** Web development (React/Node.js typical), Shopify APIs, basic e-commerce understanding, customer support skills (critical for reviews).

**Real examples:**
- **ABConvert** (A/B testing): $50K MRR, multi-million acquisition offer
- **WideBundle** (product bundling): $55-57K MRR, team of 7
- **DropCommerce** (dropshipping): $78K CAD MRR after 36 months
- **OrderlyPrint** (invoicing): $27K/month, 85% margin, solo founder
- **SuperLemon** (WhatsApp): $29K MRR, 1,700+ paying merchants

**Risks:**
- 35% of apps get zero traction (zero reviews = zero revenue)
- Shopify can build competing features natively (they've done this repeatedly)
- First 3 months median revenue is $0 — need cash reserves
- Customer support burden is heavy (merchants expect fast help)
- Shopify takes 15% after $1M lifetime revenue
- Highly competitive in popular categories

**Scores:** Speed: 2 | Solo: 3 | Defensibility: 4 | Capital: 4 | Scalability: 5

## IDEA #9: API-as-a-Service (Developer Infrastructure)

**What it is:** Build and sell an API that developers integrate into their products — image processing, data enrichment, geolocation, PDF generation, email verification, etc. Revenue compounds as customers build more deeply on your API (every line of code = switching cost).

**Revenue model:** Usage-based pricing (per API call) with monthly base subscriptions. Typical: $0 free tier + $29-$99/mo starter + $499/mo enterprise. Usage overage charges on top.

**Startup cost:** $500-$3,000 (hosting, domain, Stripe). 4-8 weeks for MVP. Serverless infrastructure (Cloudflare Workers, AWS Lambda) keeps costs near zero initially.

**Path to $10K/month (from Zuplo's 2026 API Monetization Playbook):**
- Stage 1: 50-200 customers, $50 ARPU = $2.5K-$10K MRR (months 0-6)
- Stage 2: 200-700 customers, $70 ARPU = $14K-$49K MRR (months 6-12)
- Fastest path: Find 20 customers who'd pay $500/mo, not 200 at $50/mo
- Key metric: 5-15% free-to-paid conversion rate
- Funnel math: 1,000 visitors → 20-50 signups → 10-25 API keys → 1-5 paying customers

**Revenue expansion is the secret weapon:**
- Net revenue retention >110% target — existing customers grow automatically
- Customer starting at $29/mo can grow to $200/mo as their product scales
- Usage-based pricing captures this expansion without any sales effort

**Moat:** STRONG. Every line of code integrated against your API is switching cost. Well-documented APIs with great DX create compounding lock-in. Developer trust and reliability track record.

**Skills required:** Backend development, API design, documentation writing, DevOps/infrastructure. Strongly technical — this is a developer-selling-to-developers model.

**Real examples:**
- **Bannerbear** (Jon Yongfook): Image/video generation API. $630K+ ARR, solo founder.
- **Lemon Squeezy**: Payment API for digital products. Acquired by Shopify.
- **Abstract API**: Suite of utility APIs (IP geolocation, email validation, etc.). Sold for $1M+ on Acquire.com.
- **Sidekiq** (Mike Perham): Background job processing for Ruby. $7M/year, solo founder. Open-source core + paid pro tier.

**Risks:**
- Requires strong technical skills (API design, reliability, documentation)
- Developer audience is small but demanding — poor DX = instant churn
- Infrastructure costs can surprise you at scale
- Big cloud providers can build competing services
- Long sales cycles — developers evaluate carefully before integrating

**Scores:** Speed: 2 | Solo: 3 | Defensibility: 5 | Capital: 4 | Scalability: 5

## IDEA #10: Paid Community / Membership Business

**What it is:** Build a paid online community around a specific niche, transformation, or interest. Members pay monthly/annual for access to exclusive content, live calls, peer networking, accountability, and direct access to you. Platforms: Skool, Circle, Mighty Networks, Discord.

**Revenue model:** Monthly subscription ($29-$299/mo) or annual membership ($199-$2,997/yr). Additional revenue from upsells (courses, consulting, events).

**Startup cost:** $0-$500 (Skool is $99/mo, Circle starts at $49/mo, Discord is free). Main cost is time to create initial content and build audience.

**Path to $10K/month:**
- At $49/mo → 205 members needed
- At $99/mo → 102 members needed
- At $199/mo → 51 members needed
- BusinessUnderstudy reports: "entrepreneurs making $10K-$50K/month from communities with fewer than 500 members"
- Key metrics: 3x higher LTV than courses, 85% lower churn than subscription products, 40% higher engagement than free alternatives
- Timeline: 3-9 months if you have existing audience; 6-18 months from scratch

**Moat:** Network effects (members attract members), relationships built inside community, content archive, culture/identity. STRONG moat once established — people don't leave communities where they have relationships.

**Skills required:** Community management, content creation, facilitation, niche expertise. No coding. Strong interpersonal/leadership skills critical.

**Real examples:**
- **Skool** (Alex Hormozi partnership): 13,000+ communities, many hitting $10K+/mo
- **Hampton** (Sam Parr): Peer community for founders, $8,500/yr, reportedly $10M+ ARR
- **Trends.co** (The Hustle): Business trends community, $299/yr, thousands of members
- **The Copywriter Club** (Kira Hug & Rob Marsh): Copywriting community, $97/mo, $20K+/mo
- **Indie Worldwide** (indie hacker community): Free tier + paid mastermind model

**Risks:**
- Requires constant engagement — "ghost town" communities die fast
- Hard to start from zero audience (chicken-and-egg problem)
- Member retention requires ongoing value delivery
- Platform risk if using Skool/Circle (they can change pricing/terms)
- Content treadmill — must continuously create programming
- Scaling past ~500 members requires moderators/community managers

**Scores:** Speed: 3 | Solo: 4 | Defensibility: 4 | Capital: 5 | Scalability: 3

## IDEA #12: SEO Content / Affiliate / Niche Site

**What it is:** Build a content website targeting high-intent search queries, monetized via affiliate commissions (Amazon Associates, ShareASale, impact.com), display ads (Mediavine, Raptive), or lead generation. AI tools now enable much faster content production, but Google's algorithm updates have made this riskier.

**Revenue model:** Affiliate commissions (3-50% per sale depending on program), display ads (RPM $15-$50 for US traffic), sponsored posts ($500-$5,000), lead gen fees ($10-$100+ per lead in high-value niches like insurance, finance, legal).

**Startup cost:** $100-$500 (domain, hosting, basic tools). Content production cost if outsourcing: $0.05-$0.15/word. AI-assisted content dramatically reduces this.

**Path to $10K/month:**
- **Display ads**: 300K-600K monthly pageviews at $20-$35 RPM = $6K-$21K/mo
- **Affiliate**: 50K monthly visitors × 2% conversion × $100 avg commission = $100K/mo (best case) — more realistically 50K visitors × 0.5% × $30 = $7.5K/mo
- **Lead gen**: 50K visitors × 1% conversion × $20/lead = $10K/mo
- Timeline: 6-18 months to meaningful traffic; 12-24 months to $10K/mo
- Sites in finance, health, SaaS reviews, home services historically perform best

**Moat:** Domain authority (takes years to build), topical authority in a niche, backlink profile, brand recognition. Moderate moat — hard to replicate a site with 500+ quality articles and strong DR.

**Skills required:** SEO knowledge (on-page, technical, link building), content strategy, basic WordPress/CMS skills, analytics. AI writing tools help but human editing still essential for quality.

**Real examples:**
- **Wirecutter**: Affiliate review site, acquired by NYT for $30M+
- **NerdWallet**: Finance affiliate/lead gen, IPO'd at $5B+ valuation
- **Indie niche sites**: Many solo operators earning $5K-$30K/mo documented on Niche Pursuits, Authority Hacker
- **Jake Ward**: Used programmatic SEO + AI to grow Byword.ai's traffic and revenue
- Many HCU (Helpful Content Update) victims lost 50-90% of traffic in 2023-2024

**Risks — MAJOR CONCERN IN 2025:**
- Google's Helpful Content Update (HCU) devastated many niche sites in 2023-2024
- AI Overviews in Google Search reduce click-through rates by 30-60%
- Google increasingly hostile to affiliate content and thin review sites
- AI-generated content penalties if detected or low-quality
- Revenue concentration risk (Google = single traffic source)
- Many veteran niche site operators have pivoted away from this model
- The "golden age" of affiliate sites (2015-2022) may be over

**Honest assessment:** This model is significantly riskier in 2025 than it was even 2 years ago. Only viable if you can build genuine topical authority, diversify traffic sources, or target niches where Google still sends traffic. Not recommended as a primary business — better as a supplement.

**Scores:** Speed: 1 | Solo: 4 | Defensibility: 3 | Capital: 5 | Scalability: 3
