

### Growth Trajectory (Realistic)
| Month | Revenue | Clients | Notes |
|-------|---------|---------|-------|
| 1 | $0 | 0 | Learning tools, building portfolio |
| 2 | $1,500 | 1 | First client (Upwork or referral) + setup fee |
| 3 | $3,500 | 2 | Second client, first client on retainer |
| 4 | $5,500 | 3 | LinkedIn outreach producing leads |
| 5 | $7,000 | 4 | Referral from client #1 |
| 6 | $10,000 | 5-6 | **Target hit** |

**Timeline: 4-8 months** (fastest path to $10K of any model)

### Why This Is the Fastest Path
- **Low client count needed**: 5-6 clients vs. 160 SaaS customers
- **High-touch = high retention**: Personal service creates switching costs
- **Setup fees fund growth**: $2-5K per new client covers your ramp period
- **No product to build**: You're using existing tools (Make, n8n, Zapier, LLM APIs)

### Real Benchmarks (from 2025 research)
- AI agencies commanding $300+/hr rates (Articsledge data)
- $20,000/mo retainers reported for enterprise AI consulting
- Digital marketing retainers average $3,000-$5,000/mo in 2025 (AuditSky survey)
- AI-specific retainers command 1.5-3x premium over traditional digital services
- 90% year-over-year revenue growth for AI agency sector

### Honest Assessment
**This is the most realistic path to $10K/month within 6 months for most people.** The math requires just 5-6 clients. The risk is that it's a service business — your income is directly tied to your time. To escape this, you need to either: (1) hire contractors and become a true agency, or (2) productize your services into repeatable packages with SOPs. The best AI agencies do both.

**Key risk: You're selling expertise you may not fully have yet.** The AI landscape moves fast. You need to stay current weekly. Clients will expect you to know more than them.

## MODEL #3: AI Voice Agent Service (Vertical-Specific)

### The Revenue Equation
| Package | Price/mo | Clients | Revenue |
|---------|----------|---------|---------|
| Basic AI Receptionist | $500/mo | 20 | $10,000 |
| Advanced (multi-line, CRM integration) | $1,000/mo | 10 | $10,000 |
| Enterprise (custom workflows) | $2,000/mo | 5 | $10,000 |
| **Blended realistic** | **$750/mo avg** | **14 clients** | **$10,500** |

Plus setup fees: $1,000-$3,000 per new client

### Cost Structure (at $10K/mo scale)
| Cost | Monthly |
|------|---------|
| Vapi/Bland/Synthflow platform | $200-600 |
| Per-minute voice costs (~$0.05-0.15/min) | $500-1,500 (depends on call volume) |
| Phone numbers (Twilio) | $15-50/client = $210-700 |
| CRM integrations | $50-100 |
| **Total COGS** | **$960-$2,900** |

**Gross margin: 72-91%** (per-minute costs are the variable to watch)



### Growth Trajectory
| Month | Revenue | Clients | Notes |
|-------|---------|---------|-------|
| 1 | $0 | 0 | Building demo agents, learning platform |
| 2 | $1,000 | 1 | First client (local business, setup fee) |
| 3 | $3,000 | 3 | Cold outreach to dental/vet/HVAC offices |
| 4 | $5,500 | 6 | Referrals starting, niche reputation building |
| 5 | $8,000 | 10 | LinkedIn content generating inbound |
| 6 | $10,500 | 14 | **Target hit** |

### Honest Assessment
**Strong path but narrowing window.** The per-minute costs are the variable to watch — a client with high call volume can eat margins fast. The sweet spot is businesses that get 20-50 calls/day (enough to justify AI, not enough to blow up your costs).

**Critical risk: Platform dependency.** Vapi, Bland.ai, Synthflow can change pricing, go down, or pivot. You need contingency plans.
